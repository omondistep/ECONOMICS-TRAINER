## CHAPTER ONE: PRELIMINARY PROVISIONS

## PREAMBLE

We, the grandchildren of the late Boniface Alal, united by a common lineage and shared heritage, do hereby

voluntarily associate and constitute ourselves into a Self-Help Group known as **NYIKWA BON SHG**, founded

on the principles of unity, equality, mutual support, and family solidarity.

Recognizing the importance of togetherness in promoting welfare, development, and social cohesion among

members, and desiring to strengthen our family bonds through cooperation, opportunity-sharing, and collective

responsibility, we adopt and enact this Constitution as the supreme governing instrument of the Association.

This Constitution shall guide the organization, administration, and operations of NYIKWA BON SHG in

accordance with its objectives and values as a non-political, non-discriminatory family-based association.

## ARTICLE 1: NAME AND STATUS

1.1 The name of the Association shall be **NYIKWA BON SHG**, hereinafter referred to as “the Association.”

1.2 The Association is a Self-Help Group formed for social, welfare, and developmental purposes and shall

operate as a non-political and non-profit association.

1.3 The Association shall be composed exclusively of the grandchildren of the late Boniface Alal.

## ARTICLE 2: WORDS AND EXPRESSIONS

Unless the context otherwise requires, the following words and expressions shall have the meanings assigned to

them hereunder:

a) **“Association”** means NYIKWA BON SHG.​

b) **“Member”** means any person who qualifies for and is duly registered as a member of the Association by

virtue of being a grandchild of the late Boniface Alal.​

c) **“Leadership”** means the Chairperson, Secretary, and Treasurer of the Association.​

d) **“General Meeting”** means a meeting of all registered members of the Association.​

e) **“Constitution”** means this Constitution of NYIKWA BON SHG and any lawful amendments thereto.​

f) **“Kitty”** means the collective funds of the Association derived from registration fees, contributions, or other


---


lawful sources.​

g) **“By-laws”** means subsidiary rules made under the authority of this Constitution.

## ARTICLE 3: INTERPRETATION

3.1 This Constitution shall be interpreted in a manner that promotes unity, welfare, equity, and the objectives of

the Association.

3.2 Words importing the singular shall include the plural and vice versa.

3.3 Words importing the masculine gender shall include the feminine and vice versa.

3.4 Any matter not expressly provided for under this Constitution shall be determined in accordance with the

spirit and objectives of the Association and by resolutions of the General Meeting.

## ARTICLE 4: NATURE AND CHARACTER OF THE ASSOCIATION

4.1 The Association shall be a family-based Self-Help Group formed with the primary purpose of providing a

social platform that brings together members of one lineage with unity of purpose.

4.2 The Association shall be non-political and shall not affiliate with or campaign for any political party,

movement, or candidate.

4.3 All members shall be equal before the Association and shall not be discriminated against on the basis of tribe,

age, religion, income status, or any other social distinction.

## ARTICLE 5: MAIN OBJECTIVE

5.1 The main objective of the Association shall be to promote unity, welfare, and development among members

through collective participation, mutual support, and the strengthening of family ties.

## ARTICLE 6: SPECIFIC OBJECTIVES

Without prejudice to the generality of Article 5, the specific objectives of the Association shall be to:


---


a) Provide a social platform for members to interact, bond, and nurture family unity.​

b) Promote welfare and development through sharing of opportunities, knowledge, and resources.​

c) Encourage mutual assistance in times of need, including health-related challenges and bereavement.​

d) Facilitate collaboration among members in areas such as employment, business, construction, marketing,

legal representation, and other professional or technical services.​

e) Strengthen family identity, values, and collective responsibility across generations.​

f) Foster a culture of accountability, transparency, and cooperation in the management of common affairs.

## ARTICLE 7: MEMORANDUM OF ASSOCIATION

7.1 The Association is established as a voluntary Self-Help Group with no intention of generating profit for

distribution among members.

7.2 All income, contributions, and assets of the Association shall be applied solely toward the promotion of its

objectives as set out in this Constitution.

7.3 The Association shall exist in perpetuity unless dissolved in accordance with the provisions of this

Constitution.


---


## CHAPTER TWO: MEMBERSHIP

## ARTICLE 8: ELIGIBILITY FOR MEMBERSHIP

8.1 Membership of the Association shall be open exclusively to persons who are grandchildren of the late

Boniface Alal.

8.2 Membership shall be acquired by virtue of lineage and shall be deemed a lifetime membership subject to

compliance with this Constitution and the by-laws of the Association.

8.3 No person shall be denied membership on the basis of tribe, age, religion, gender, or income status, provided

that such person satisfies the requirement under Article 8.1.

## ARTICLE 9: REGISTRATION OF MEMBERS

9.1 Every eligible person seeking to be recognized as a member of the Association shall register his or her name

with the Secretary in the prescribed manner.

9.2 Upon registration, a member shall pay a non-refundable registration fee of Kenya Shillings Three Hundred

(KES 300), which shall form part of the Association’s kitty as a sign of commitment to the Association.

9.3 The Secretary shall maintain an official Register of Members containing the names and relevant particulars

of all registered members.

## ARTICLE 10: RIGHTS OF MEMBERS

10.1 Every registered member shall have the right to:

a) Participate in all meetings and activities of the Association.​

b) Vote and be voted for in accordance with this Constitution.​

c) Access welfare support and opportunities made available through the Association.​

d) Receive information on the affairs and financial status of the Association.​

e) Propose matters for discussion at meetings of the Association.

10.2 All members shall enjoy equal rights and privileges within the Association.


---


## ARTICLE 11: DUTIES AND OBLIGATIONS OF MEMBERS

11.1 Every member shall have the duty to:

a) Uphold and comply with this Constitution and the by-laws of the Association.​

b) Promote unity, harmony, and mutual respect among members.​

c) Participate in the activities and meetings of the Association.​

d) Contribute such amounts as may be lawfully required for the welfare and activities of the Association.​

e) Protect the good name, property, and interests of the Association.

11.2 Members shall conduct themselves in a manner that reflects the values and objectives of the Association.

## ARTICLE 12: WELFARE ENTITLEMENTS

12.1 Members shall be entitled to benefit from opportunities shared by fellow members, including but not

limited to employment opportunities, partnerships, and access to professional or technical expertise.

12.2 In the event that a registered member suffers a major medical condition or undergoes a major medical

procedure, the Association shall, subject to available resources, mobilize members to provide financial, material,

or moral support.

12.3 In the event of the death of a registered member, the Association shall rally members to provide support to

the affected family in accordance with the by-laws and resolutions of the Association.

## ARTICLE 13: DISCIPLINE AND TERMINATION OF MEMBERSHIP

13.1 Membership may be suspended or terminated only on the following grounds:

a) Proven misconduct or actions prejudicial to the objectives or reputation of the Association.​

b) Persistent refusal or neglect to comply with this Constitution or lawful by-laws.​

c) Voluntary resignation submitted in writing to the Secretary.

13.2 No member shall be suspended or removed without being given a fair opportunity to be heard.

13.3 A decision to suspend or terminate membership shall be made by resolution of the General Meeting or as

may be provided for in the by-laws.


---


## ARTICLE 14: RE-ADMISSION

14.1 A member who resigns or whose membership is terminated may apply for re-admission in accordance with

procedures prescribed by the by-laws.

14.2 Re-admission shall be subject to approval by the General Meeting and such conditions as it may impose.


---


## CHAPTER THREE: LEADERSHIP AND MANAGEMENT

## ARTICLE 15: STRUCTURE OF LEADERSHIP

15.1 The leadership of the Association shall consist of the following officers:

a) Chairperson​

b) Secretary​

c) Treasurer

15.2 The officers shall be elected from among the registered members of the Association in accordance with this

Constitution.

15.3 The officers shall collectively be referred to as “the Leadership” of the Association and shall be responsible

for the day-to-day administration and management of the affairs of the Association.

## ARTICLE 16: TENURE OF OFFICE

16.1 The term of office for all elected leaders shall be determined by the General Meeting and shall be subject to

re-election.

16.2 A leader may resign from office by giving written notice to the Secretary or, in the case of the Secretary, to

the Chairperson.

16.3 A leader may be removed from office for gross misconduct, incompetence, or breach of this Constitution

by a resolution of the General Meeting, provided that such leader shall be given a fair opportunity to be heard.

## ARTICLE 17: POWERS AND FUNCTIONS OF THE CHAIRPERSON

17.1 The Chairperson shall:

a) Be the principal head and spokesperson of the Association.​

b) Preside over all meetings of the Association and ensure orderly conduct of business.​

c) Provide general oversight and direction in the implementation of the objectives of the Association.​

d) Represent the Association in external engagements where authorized by the General Meeting.​


---


e) Perform any other duties as may be assigned by the General Meeting or as provided for under this

Constitution.

## ARTICLE 18: POWERS AND FUNCTIONS OF THE SECRETARY

18.1 The Secretary shall:

a) Be the chief administrative officer of the Association.​

b) Keep and maintain accurate records of members in the Register of Members.​

c) Issue notices convening meetings of the Association in accordance with this Constitution.​

d) Record and keep minutes of all meetings of the Association.​

e) Maintain custody of all official documents and records of the Association.​

f) Handle all official correspondence of the Association.​

g) Perform any other duties as may be assigned by the Leadership or the General Meeting.

## ARTICLE 19: POWERS AND FUNCTIONS OF THE TREASURER

19.1 The Treasurer shall:

a) Be the chief financial officer of the Association.

b) Receive, keep, and account for all monies belonging to the Association.​

c) Ensure that all funds of the Association are deposited in a manner approved by the Leadership.​

d) Keep proper books of accounts and financial records of the Association.​

e) Prepare and present financial reports to the members as required under this Constitution.​

f) Perform any other financial duties as may be assigned by the Leadership or the General Meeting.

## ARTICLE 20: FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT AND ACCOUNTABILITY

20.1 All monies of the Association shall be used solely for the promotion of the objectives of the Association.

20.2 The Treasurer shall provide monthly financial updates to the members indicating the status of the kitty,

including income and expenditure.

20.3 No funds shall be withdrawn or expended without the knowledge and approval of the Leadership in

accordance with procedures set out in the by-laws.


---


20.4 Proper financial records shall be kept at all times and shall be made available for inspection by any member

upon reasonable request.

20.5 The financial year of the Association shall be determined by the General Meeting.

## ARTICLE 21: COLLECTIVE RESPONSIBILITY

21.1 The Leadership shall be collectively responsible to the General Meeting for the proper management of the

affairs, finances, and property of the Association.

21.2 The Leadership shall act in good faith and in the best interests of the Association at all times.


---


## CHAPTER FOUR: MEETINGS

## ARTICLE 22: MODES OF MEETINGS

22.1 The Association shall conduct its business through the following meetings:

a) Monthly Meetings​

b) Annual General Meeting (AGM)​

c) Special General Meetings

22.2 Meetings may be held physically, virtually, or in a hybrid format as may be determined by the Leadership,

provided that all members are duly notified.

## ARTICLE 23: MONTHLY MEETINGS

23.1 Ordinary meetings of the Association shall be held on the First Sunday of every month at such time and

place as shall be communicated by the Secretary.

23.2 The Leadership may, where necessary and for purposes of socialization or welfare coordination, convene

additional meetings every fortnight, provided that members are duly informed.

23.3 The quorum for a Monthly Meeting shall be such number of members as may be prescribed in the by-laws

or, in the absence thereof, one-third (1/3) of the registered members.

## ARTICLE 24: ANNUAL GENERAL MEETING (AGM)

24.1 The Association shall hold an Annual General Meeting once every calendar year at a time and place

appointed by the Leadership.

24.2 The AGM shall be convened for the purposes of:

a) Receiving and considering reports from the Chairperson, Secretary, and Treasurer.​

b) Reviewing the financial statements of the Association.​

c) Electing or confirming the Leadership where applicable.​

d) Considering and approving by-laws and major policy decisions.​

e) Deliberating on any other business lawfully brought before the meeting.


---


24.3 The Secretary shall give members at least three (3) months’ notice of the AGM.

24.4 The quorum for the AGM shall be not less than one-half (1/2) of the registered members.

## ARTICLE 25: SPECIAL GENERAL MEETINGS

25.1 A Special General Meeting may be convened:

a) By the Leadership where urgent or special matters arise; or

b) Upon a written request signed by not less than one-third (1/3) of the registered members.

25.2 The Secretary shall give at least seven (7) days’ notice specifying the purpose of the Special General Meeting.

25.3 No business other than that specified in the notice shall be transacted at a Special General Meeting.

## ARTICLE 26: VOTING

26.1 Every registered member shall be entitled to one vote.

26.2 Voting shall be by show of hands unless a virtual poll is demanded by a majority of members present.

26.3 Decisions of the Association shall be made by a simple majority of members present and voting, except

where this Constitution provides otherwise.

26.4 In the event of a tie, the Chairperson shall have a casting vote.

## ARTICLE 27: RESOLUTIONS

27.1 All resolutions passed at any meeting of the Association shall be binding on all members.

27.2 Resolutions shall be properly recorded in the minutes of the meeting and kept by the Secretary as part of

the official records of the Association.


---


## ARTICLE 28: ORDER OF BUSINESS

28.1 The order of business at meetings shall be as prescribed by the by-laws or as determined by the Leadership.

28.2 Matters not on the agenda may be discussed with the consent of the majority of members present


---


## CHAPTER FIVE: BY-LAWS AND ACTIVITIES OF THE ASSOCIATION

## ARTICLE 29: POWER TO MAKE BY-LAWS

29.1 The Leadership shall have power to make, issue, amend, or rescind by-laws, rules, and regulations for the

proper control, administration, and management of the Association’s operations, finances, affairs, interests,

effects, and property.

29.2 All by-laws made under this Article shall be consistent with and subject to this Constitution.

29.3 Any by-laws, rules, or regulations made shall, in the opinion of the members, be necessary or desirable for

the effective governance and functioning of the Association.

## ARTICLE 30: APPROVAL OF BY-LAWS

30.1 No by-law shall take effect unless and until it has been approved by the members of the Association

through a poll or resolution passed by a simple majority of members present and voting at a duly convened

General Meeting.

30.2 The Secretary shall circulate proposed by-laws to members prior to the meeting at which they are to be

considered.

30.3 Approved by-laws shall be binding upon all members of the Association.

## ARTICLE 31: AMENDMENT OF BY-LAWS

31.1 By-laws may be amended or repealed by a resolution of the General Meeting passed by a simple majority of

members present and voting.

31.2 Any amendment to the by-laws shall be recorded in writing and kept as part of the official records of the

Association.


---


## ARTICLE 32: ACTIVITIES OF THE ASSOCIATION

32.1 The Association shall undertake activities that are lawful and consistent with its objectives as set out in this

Constitution.

32.2 Without prejudice to the generality of Article 32.1, the activities of the Association shall include:

a) Organizing annual gatherings for bonding and social engagement among members.​

b) Facilitating regular meetings for interaction, planning, and coordination of welfare matters.​

c) Promoting opportunity-sharing among members, including employment, business, and professional

opportunities.​

d) Supporting members during times of need, including illness, major medical procedures, and bereavement.​

e) Encouraging cooperation and collective action in family development initiatives.

## ARTICLE 33: NON-POLITICAL CHARACTER OF ACTIVITIES

33.1 All activities of the Association shall be strictly non-political.

33.2 The Association shall not participate in, fund, or promote any political party, political campaign, or

political ideology.

## ARTICLE 34: SOCIAL AND WELFARE ENGAGEMENTS

34.1 The Association shall prioritize activities that strengthen family ties, unity, and collective responsibility.

34.2 Social and welfare engagements shall be conducted in a manner that respects the equality, dignity, and

rights of all members.


---


## CHAPTER SIX: AMENDMENTS AND DISSOLUTION

## ARTICLE 35: AMENDMENT OF THE CONSTITUTION

35.1 This Constitution may be amended only by a resolution of the General Meeting of the Association.

35.2 Any proposal to amend this Constitution shall be submitted in writing to the Secretary at least fourteen

(14) days before the meeting at which it is to be considered.

35.3 The Secretary shall circulate the proposed amendment to all members prior to the meeting.

35.4 An amendment to this Constitution shall be deemed duly adopted if approved by not less than two-thirds

(2/3) of the members present and voting at a duly convened General Meeting.

35.5 No amendment shall be valid if it is inconsistent with the main objectives and principles of the Association

as set out in this Constitution.

## ARTICLE 36: SUPREMACY OF THE CONSTITUTION

36.1 This Constitution shall be the supreme governing instrument of the Association.

36.2 In the event of any conflict between this Constitution and any by-laws, rules, or regulations made under it,

the provisions of this Constitution shall prevail to the extent of the inconsistency.

## ARTICLE 37: VOLUNTARY DISSOLUTION OF THE ASSOCIATION

37.1 The Association may be dissolved voluntarily by a resolution of the General Meeting.

37.2 A proposal for dissolution shall be submitted in writing to the Secretary and shall be communicated to all

members at least thirty (30) days prior to the meeting convened to consider the matter.

37.3 The resolution for dissolution shall require the approval of not less than two-thirds (2/3) of the registered

members present and voting at a duly convened General Meeting.


---


## ARTICLE 38: EFFECT OF DISSOLUTION

38.1 Upon dissolution of the Association:

a) All liabilities of the Association shall first be settled and discharged.​

b) Any remaining funds or property of the Association shall not be distributed among members for personal

benefit but shall be applied in a manner consistent with the objectives of the Association or donated to a

charitable cause as may be resolved by the General Meeting.​

c) The books, records, and official documents of the Association shall be properly archived or disposed of in a

manner approved by the General Meeting.

38.2 The Leadership shall be responsible for supervising the winding-up process in accordance with resolutions

of the General Meeting.

## ARTICLE 39: CONTINUITY OF FAMILY UNITY

39.1 Notwithstanding dissolution, members shall continue to uphold the values of unity, mutual respect, and

family solidarity that informed the establishment of the Association.

39.2 Dissolution shall not extinguish the moral and social obligations of members to support one another as a

family of common lineage.
